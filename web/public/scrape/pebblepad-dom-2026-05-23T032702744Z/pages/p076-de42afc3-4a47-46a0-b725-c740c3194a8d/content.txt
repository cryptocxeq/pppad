Unit 10 Step 2
Observing Learning for Evaluating our Effectiveness- Pupil Progress.
Learning Outcomes
Critically explore a range of philosophies of education and evidence of the potential impact of these on educational provision and the experiences of learners in different contexts
Demonstrate a critical, evidence -based understanding of learner development and how teachers can: assess individual needs, plan effective teaching and support which promotes high expectations and progress for all learners; and assess and evaluate the effectiveness of this work.
Activities
This activity uses observation of a pupils again, but with a different motivation. The aim is to evaluate your teaching and to think about pupil progress. You may want to remind yourself of the content of the session in Unit 1 Step 5.
Part 1.
Record a lesson using Panopto. Answer the following questions on the Padlet.
How do I know how effective my teaching has been?
What evidence do I have for this?
How do I keep records of this?
What provision do I make for those whose learning has not been as full as other pupils?
How do my assessments feed into my future planning?
Part 2.
Use one of the  observation sheets below to carry out another observation on one pupil in your class/session or ask a pupil to fill in the feedback sheets. You could choose a pupil who is not attaining as well as others. Please do not put the pupil’s name on the sheet.
After the observation consider:
Was the pupil on task during the session, if so, why and if not, why?
What was their understanding of the lesson outcomes, purpose and links to the rest of the curriculum?
What were they most interested in?
What did they think would have improved their learning?
What do you think could have improved the pupil's learning, perhaps drawing on some of the effective learning strategies in unit 4 and 5, if you repeated the same lesson/session?
Keep notes on the observation. They will be useful to you in the practice module.
