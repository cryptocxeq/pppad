Unit 2 Step 1
What do I think about learning?
This unit ask you to consider how you think children learn. It explores some learning theories and asks you to analyse some teaching to reflect on the learning theories that underpin the actions of the teacher.
​​​​​​​
Learning Outcomes:
To demonstrate a critical, evidence-based understanding of learner development
Activity
This activity is designed to start you thinking about how you believe pupils learn.
Read the statements below.  Sort them into those you agree with, those you don’t and those you are not sure about. Put them on the Padlet  in the three columns in your own colour and look at what others think. Are they the same as yours?
Why do you think teachers have different views on learning ?
These notes will start your thinking on your presentation and written assignment.
Statements about learning:
Pupils are empty containers that need to be filled with knowledge.
Pupils learn best when they understand what the learning is for.
Pupils need to learn from each other as that is the way they will work in the future
If you repeat something enough times pupils will learn it.
Some pupils are just born with less intelligence than others, whatever you do it will not make a difference
Tell them and pupils forget, teach them and they may remember, involve them and they learn.
Pupils learn by building on what they already know
Some pupils come from backgrounds which means they find learning easier than others.
Learning should be about sharing the joy of learning with your pupils
Pupils should leave their home issues behind when they come to study
