Unit 5 Step 4
Auditing our own Teaching and Questioning.
Learning Outcomes
Evaluate their own personal values, beliefs and experiences and consider the implications of these in their roles as educator
Activity
Questioning can be the most valuable strategy you use in the classroom. It can encourage pupils to think more critically, identify diverse ideas, extend their thinking and help them see different ways to proceed.
1. Audit one of your own lessons. You could do this by recording yourself on your phone ( if school allows phones in classroom). Be sure to delete after use and to record your voice and not that of the pupils.
Consider:
How many open/closed questions did you use – is the balance right?
Are you playing guess what the teacher is thinking of?
What do you do with incorrect responses – move on from, ignore, develop or ridicule?
Can you employ some of the lessons learnt in this topic?
Set yourself a target to work on in the future:
For example;
Scaffold answers for weaker pupils
Utilise a hinge questions (see below)
Increase take up/thinking time.
Make notes on your experiences of your questioning below and bring to the next live session.
