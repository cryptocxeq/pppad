Both Vygotsky and Dewey reject ed didactic models of teaching for children, instead highlighting the importance of play and learning through experience and ones’ environment . Though both authors seem similar in many respects, there are marked difference between their theories, but differences I believe can be balanced carefully in the ELT, CLT multi-cultural classroom. Vygotsky saw the classroom as ‘social organisation… representative of the larger social community’, with social organisation and community as the agent of change for the individual. Education serves to build children into the community and become productive members of the community, with integration of social and cultural goals of importance in social pedagogy. While Dewey also saw the classroom as a social organisation ‘representative of the larger social community’, however it is the child that is the agent of change for social organisation (Glassman 2001, p13). While Vygotsky was recognising a more collectivist perspective, Dewey was promoting the individual; I see both of these philosophies reflected in my practice, primarily because I have been educated within an individualistic culture in the UK, but now find myself navigating education in a collectivist culture. I strive to bring out the individual in the classroom, and celebrate difference of all types, while also acknowledging the dominant communist/ socialist ideologies and philosophies that students are being schooled under in their regular/ public schools. These are not ideologies I separate from the classroom , but invite should it be part of relevant discussion.
