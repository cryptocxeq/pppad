Unit 7 Step 1
Assessment and Assessment for Learning.
Unit 7 studies assessment and how it can be used to support pupils' learning as well as inform the teacher, parents and outside agencies.
Learning Outcomes
Articulate a critical engagement with literature relating to a variety of contemporary, best practice international primary secondary pedagogical approaches and frameworks, including the NC and their contexts, recognising their potential implications for learning and teaching in international contexts
Activities
1. Think about a time where you felt you were successfully assessed. What was the mode of assessment and how was it conducted?
How did you know about the criteria on which you were going to be assessed?
2. The video introduces you to Assessment and Assessment for Learning (AfL) . It covers:
What is assessment, why we assess, assessment for learning, strategies for AfL?
Watch the video and make notes considering:
How do we know what pupils have learnt?
How can we find out?
What strategies work?
What do we do with our assessment information?

A4L “ Assessment for learning is the process of seeking and interpreting evidence for use by learners and their teachers to decide where the learners are in their learning, where they need to go and how best to get there”. Assessment Reform Group, 2002

Assessment for Learning Research The work of Black and Wiliams surveying 250 studies on raising achievement (1998) demonstrated how useful formative assessment is to children’s progress in schools especially careful questioning .
