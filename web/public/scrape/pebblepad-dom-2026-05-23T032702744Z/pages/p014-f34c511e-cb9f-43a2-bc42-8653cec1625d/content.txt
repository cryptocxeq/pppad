University Quality Procedures.
When a new course is proposed it is scrutinised by committee to see if it aligns with the national standards for higher education.
Each module is reviewed by students at the end of each block of teaching.
10% of all assignments are second marked and the staff have a benchmarking meeting before marking begins.
6 assignments from each module are sent to an external examiner from another university to check the feedback and standards.
These results, as well as data from the grades and submissions student's make, are reviewed by a programme committee.
The marks and statistics from the modules go to exam board where they are ratified and the external examiner will feedback on the course and assignments.
At periodic intervals, the university will review courses and check they still match their proposed learning intentions and module specifications.
Extensions are only granted in certain situations. These are not granted by tutors. Please contact infopoint@uwe.ac.uk and book an appointment with a student advisor.
